Why window tinting services matter in summer

Extended sun exposure does more than make your car uncomfortable; it can have long-term effects on both your health and your vehicle. Even inside your car, prolonged heat and UV exposure can raise cabin temperatures to unsafe levels, increasing the risk of heat-related illnesses, skin damage, and eye strain. The sun’s glare can also affect visibility and make driving more dangerous.

In addition to health concerns, excessive heat can fade your upholstery, crack your dashboard, and force your AC system to work harder, leading to higher fuel consumption and unnecessary wear on your vehicle. Over time, these issues can lower your car’s resale value.

Installing the right window tint is a practical solution. A high-quality window tint blocks harmful UV rays, reduces interior heat, and minimizes glare. It not only protects your car’s interior, but it also helps create a safer, more comfortable environment for you and your passengers.

What makes a good window tint for heat rejection?

A professionally installed window tint blocks up to 99% of harmful UV rays that can damage your skin and vehicle interior:

Not all window tints perform equally. The best films for heat reduction share several qualities:

  • High infrared (IR) heat rejection: IR radiation is the main contributor to heat from sunlight. Premium films can reject over 80–90% of IR.
  • Low visible light transmission (VLT) (optional): Darker tints usually block more sunlight, but high-quality films can reject heat even with high VLT (i.e., they stay light while still rejecting heat).
  • UV protection: Quality tints block up to 99% of UV rays, which not only protects your skin and eyes but also keeps your interior cooler.
  • Long-term durability and clarity: Look for films with long-term warranties and resistance to fading, bubbling, or discoloration.

Not all window tints have the same capacity when it comes to beating the heat. Choose high-quality films that offer excellent heat rejection, crystal-clear clarity, and a reliable warranty for long-term performance and peace of mind.

Types of car window tint and their heat-blocking performance

1. Dyed window tint

Dyed film is a traditional choice due to its low cost, but when it comes to heat rejection, it offers only basic protection. It can reduce some glare and UV exposure, but it doesn’t significantly lower cabin temperatures.

2. Metalized window tint

Metalized films contain tiny metallic particles that reflect heat and UV rays. They’re more effective than dyed film, but they may interfere with cell signals and GPS systems, something to consider if you rely heavily on your phone for navigation.

3. Carbon window tint

Carbon films offer better insulation and won’t fade over time. These tints effectively block infrared rays, which are responsible for heat buildup. They’re a strong middle-ground choice for those wanting heat reduction and longevity without breaking the bank.

4. Ceramic window tint – the best choice for heat reduction

If you’re looking for the most effective solution, ceramic window tint is widely regarded as the best option. It blocks up to 99% of harmful UV rays and significantly reduces infrared heat, all while maintaining excellent visibility. Unlike metalized films, ceramic tints won’t interfere with electronics.
